# Script

## Tone & writing style

Keep all edits and additions consistent with the style below.

**Audience & platform**
- Written for milovana.com tease players — an audience already familiar with metronome/edging teases.
- The novelty being sold is *hands-free*: the metronome they'd normally follow themselves instead drives a real machine.
- Lead with the fantasy, not the technology. The hook is surrender of control ("you don't have to move").

**Voice**
- **Spoken by a seductive female persona** — the model shown in the images. She speaks in the **first person** ("I'll take it from here") and addresses the player directly as "you", as if she's the one guiding the experience. Even practical pages are framed as her talking the player through it.
- **Sensual and suggestive, not crude.** Prefer "drives / lingers / let it" over blunt words like "pounds / take it." The title keeps the word "fucking" (it's the product framing), but her words stay seductive.
- **The player is gender-neutral** — no anatomy assumptions about *them*. (The narrator is female; the listener is anyone.)
- Warm, intimate, and inviting; a light, playful wink is welcome (e.g. "...and let me take over ;)").
- Build anticipation; reassure rather than instruct sternly — she's in control, but she wants the player to feel safe and looked after.

**Structure & pacing**
- One job per page — short, tappable beats rather than dense screens.
- Alternate registers cleanly: *mood/voice text* for immersion, plain practical copy for setup/safety. Don't blend the two on the same line.
- Practical pages (requirements, how-it-works, safety) stay tight and skimmable — icon bullets, short steps, numbered when sequential.

**Formatting conventions**
- Each `## Page N` heading marks a new page in the tease.
- Lines in _italics_ are mood/voice text shown over the image.
- `[ ... ]` are author notes, not shown to the player.
- `[IMAGE: <bucket>/*]` is an image-placement note (not shown to the player): it names the **source bucket and scene intent** for that beat. `[IMAGE: <bucket>/hero — desc]` and the single-image buckets (`title-hero`, `climax-hero`) name an exact shot; `[IMAGE: hold]` keeps the previously shown image (it persists across pages until replaced).

[Author note — image selection: this tease uses **tailored, hand-picked images**, not random play-time selection. Each `[IMAGE: <bucket>/*]` marker names the **source bucket + scene intent**; at authoring (workflow step 9) every marker is resolved to a *specific* `gallery:<uuid>/<id>` locator chosen to fit that beat's text, using the vision tags in `asset-content.json`. Consequence: each bucket needs **one tailored image per placement** (not a padded random pool), and the vision-tagging pass must describe images well enough to match them to scenes.]
